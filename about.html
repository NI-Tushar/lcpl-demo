Director of LCPL

Logistics Construction Private Limited (LCPL) is a full locally owned construction firm in Singapore.

LCPL has stepped into business as a partnership in 1985. Since our incorporation in 25th April 1992, the company had been dealing with various kinds of construction works such as:

  • Building Construction Works.
  • Addition and Alteration Works.
  • Upgrading Works.
  • Interior Decoration Works.
  • Loose Furniture & Built-in Fitments.

LCPL’s core business lies in general building but we are also aggressively involved in interior and furniture works. LCPL operates a carpentry workshop at No.24 Kranji Road, Singapore 739465 which has its own state of art technology. Fabrication of all types of furniture and fitments are done in- house. Carpenters employed are highly experienced and well-versed.

To date, LCPL has been serving the community for nearly four decades and we are still striving our best to continue.

Our Vision

LCPL has been established strongly in the construction industry for 35 years in Singapore. With its steady development and growth in the Construction line, LCPL vision for the next 5 to 10 years will be set as follows:

  • To maintain current working etiquettes in providing quality end product to the users.
  • To enhance the overall working system in order to compete with the current fast-paced industry.
  • To become a Total Integrated Turnkey General Construction & Interior Decoration Contractor offering quality workmanship, speed, service and competitive prices.
  • To maintain accident free and a Healthy Job Site Environment and hence achieving 100% Legal and Regulatory Compliance.

Logistics Construction A subsidiary of Boldtek Holdings Limited SGX-Catalist SGX-5VI

Boldtek Holdings Limited is principally engaged to provide building construction services, interior decoration and fit-out services in Singapore. It also operates a precast manufacturing plant in the Iskandar region of Johor, Malaysia. The products from this precast plant primarily serve the Group’s public and private residential, industrial and commercial projects in Singapore. The plant’s excess capacity, if any, can be channelled to customers outside Singapore — possibly those in the Iskandar region.

Its indirect wholly owned subsidiaries, Le Premier Development Pte. Ltd. Or “Le Premier Singapore” and Le Premier Development Sdn. Bhd. or “Le Premier Malaysia”, are engaged in construction development. Le Premier Singapore is currently redeveloping a freehold residential site located at 21 Paya Lebar Crescent, acquired in 2013, into high-end landed cluster housing. Le Premier Malaysia is currently developing a plot of freehold land in Senai Industrial Park, Johor, Malaysia, acquired in 2014, into medium industrial factories.

The Company also has two indirect subsidiaries, an indirect 60% interest in New Soil Technologies Pte. Ltd. or “New Soil” and 100% in MSC Engineering Pte. Ltd. or “MSC”. New Soil partners in soil investigation and treatment activities, while MSC supports building and construction activities.
